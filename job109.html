Southwest Airlines Remote Job: Technology SCM Category Manager

Date: November 23, 2024

We are committed to providing our Employees with a stable work environment with equal opportunity for learning and personal growth. Creativity and innovation are encouraged for improving the effectiveness of Southwest Airlines. Above all, Employees will be provided the same concern, respect, and caring attitude within the organization that they are expected to share externally with every Southwest Customer.

Job Description:

Southwest firmly believes that our People are our greatest asset, and Leaders within the Company support our People by ensuring all have equal opportunity for learning and personal growth. The Technology Supply Chain Management (SCM) Category Manager joins the Supply Chain Management Infrastructure & Services Team, supporting the Technology Infrastructure & Services Group, which includes Network and Infrastructure Engineering, Field Services, and Quality Management Office (QMO). The Team provides Supply Chain support for Southwest data centers, servers, and telecommunications services. A typical day includes managing 10-15 efforts, including negotiating master agreements and statements of work, managing suppliers, and educating stakeholders on SCM processes.

Additional Details:

  • This role is offered as a regional workplace position, requiring work from a mix of on-site and remote offices as needed.
  • Ineligible locations: Alaska, Delaware, New Jersey, North Dakota, South Dakota, Vermont, West Virginia, Wyoming.
  • U.S. citizenship or current authorization to work in the U.S. is required. No work authorization sponsorship available.

Southwest Airlines is an Equal Opportunity Employer.

Job Summary:

The Technology SCM Category Manager will manage a portfolio and specific categories of related technology suppliers, supporting complex and high-value contracts. The role involves advising on common risks, managing strategic sourcing events, negotiating contracts, and collaborating with internal customers to ensure suppliers meet business objectives.

Responsibilities:

  • Advise technology and stakeholders on supplier agreements, risks, and program/project issues.
  • Assist leaders with the decision-making process through competitive evaluations of new suppliers.
  • Create and implement negotiation strategies and manage supplier communications.
  • Collaborate with internal customers to understand business requirements and set expectations.
  • Identify procurement opportunities through data analysis and discussions with stakeholders.
  • Communicate performance and concerns with suppliers to redefine relationships when necessary.
